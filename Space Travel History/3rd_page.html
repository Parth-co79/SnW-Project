The Space Race (1945–1975)

After World War II, the two new superpowers — the United States and the Soviet Union — began a fierce competition not just on Earth but in space. This 30-year period became one of the most thrilling chapters in human history, transforming rocket weapons into peaceful tools of exploration.

The Cold War (post-1945) created a global rivalry between the capitalist USA and the communist USSR.Each side wanted to prove its technological superiority and military power.Space became the perfect stage: whoever mastered it could show global dominance.

After the war, both nations captured German rocket technology and engineers.In the USA, Wernher von Braun and his team began developing improved versions of the V-2 rocket.In the USSR, engineers led by Sergey Korolev rebuilt German designs and quickly developed their own large-scale rockets.These rival programs laid the groundwork for space launch vehicles instead of just missiles.
